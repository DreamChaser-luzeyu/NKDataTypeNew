Chapter 1, Exercise 9

(a)
The maximum currency amount that can be represented is $x.99 and the minimum is -$x.99, where x is 232-1. However, if we plan to do any arithmetic on the currency amounts, then the limits are changed because our codes combine the dollar and cents amounts into a single long by multiplying the dollar amount by 100 and then adding in the number of cents. When arithmetic is to be performed, the absolute value of the currency amount $x.yy should be such that 100x + y <= 232-1.
(b)
When the data type of dollars is changed to int, the maximum currency amount that can be represented is $32,768.99 and the minimum amount is -$32,768.99 (note that one of the 16 bits is reserved for a sign even though we never store a negative number).
(c)
See answer to (a).
